Introduction¶

This dataset has information from over 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of no show appointments of patients for their appointment. There are many attributes about the patient are included in this dataset.This project aims to investigate this dataset and give insight on the correlation of the attributes provided.

I will be looking at the following questions:

  • No show appointment is more in which age group
  • Which Gender has the most no show for appointment
  • Which appointment day has most No Show appointment
  • which neighbourhood had the most No Show appointment
  • The correlation of Patients conditions or sickness with No show appointment
  • Percentage of Female and Male Gender in the Dataset

Columns of the dataset According to the dataset description, 14 columns of data were recorded as following:

  • PatientId : ID of the patient
  • AppointmentID : Identification of each appointment
  • Gender : Male or Female .
  • ScheduledDay : The day someone called or booked the appointment.
  • AppointmentDay : The day of the actual appointment.
  • Age : How old is the patient took place.
  • Scholarship : Brazilian bursary to poor families.
  • Hipertension : Condition if patient suffers from high blood pressure.
  • Diabetes : Condition if patient is diabetes.
  • Alcoholism : Condition If patient is considered alcoholic.
  • Handcap : Condition if patient has disability(ies),level of disability from 0 to 5 is recorded
  • SMS_received : Number of SMS messages sent to patient.
  • No-show : Condition if the patient didn't show, Yes means the patient did not show and vice versa

Findings from the Dataset¶

Conclusion¶

  • From my findings, I observed that there are more female gender in the dataset.About 60% of the gender are females
  • There is a strong correlation between the gender and 'no-show' of appointment.I observed that more females missed their appointment.This might be due partly to the large number of female data collected.
  • The age has a correlation with the 'no-show' column, the average age of those who missed their appointment is within 35 years, which is within the mean age as seen from the data.However, more data of people within the age range of 30 to 50 were used in this dataset.I also noticed that the max age of participant in this data is about 115 years old.

  • I also observed that the recorded sickness or conditions all have negative correlation with 'no-show' of appointment.As observed, most of the people involded in this dataset who missed their appointments were not handicapped or sick of the recorded illnesses.

  • One limitation I observeed is that the dataset has no information on population of the neighbourhood as at the time of the data collection. Hence, one can not really compare the neighbourhoods based on the information gathered from this dataset,because the population of each area might affect the inference from this dataset.For example, a neighbourhood of two million population is expected to have more people going for medical appointments compare with neighbourhood of two hundred thousand.Hence, it will be unfair to compare both neighbourhoods without consideration to their population size in the data analysis.
